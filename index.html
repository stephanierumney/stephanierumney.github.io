My name is Stephanie

Throughout my 22 year teaching career, I have taught in both mainstream and special schools, working with learners who have Specific Learning Difficulties (including dyslexia, dyspraxia, dysgraphia, dyscalculia), Autism, ADHD, Profound and Multiple Learning Difficulties, and Severe Learning Difficulties.

Many of these individuals had complex needs and conditions that required a differentiated National Curriculum.

During the last six years of my career, since working independently, I have also been supporting adults who have needed additional learning support and study skills training to help manage their studies.

I am also a registered Non-Medical Helper with the DSA.

More about me
As an individual who was diagnosed with dyslexia at 41 years old, and coming from an incredibly neuro-diverse family, I fully appreciate how learning differences can significantly impact an individual’s experience of their education and how it can affect their emotional and mental well-being while in education and during their working and home life.

School is not the only place an individual can get access to support. Through my role as an assessor for dyslexia and as a SpLD teacher, my working philosophy is to aim to engage both children and adults; helping unlock their potential and ensure they understand any learning differences they may have.

It's important to learn how to live with them, use them to their advantage, and learn strategies to help them succeed and progress in their learning and career journey.

I currently hold an Assessors Practicing Certificate (APC) with PATOSS this has to be renewed every three years, during this period it is expected by the awarding body that I complete up to 30 hours of CPD (5 hours SASC approved) each year.

As a member of The Dyslexia Guild, the Northamptonshire and Buckinghamshire Dyslexia Association (NBDA) and the Northamptonshire PATOSS Group, I am able to stay abreast of changes to practices and standards.
Full Diagnostic Assessment
  • Approximately 3 hours, this can only be completed on individuals either age 8+, or those that have already entered year 3.
  • Assessment consists of standardised assessments which reflect strengths and challenges across underlying ability and cognitive processing abilities.
  • Assessment of attainment in the areas of reading, spelling and writing.
  • Detailed diagnostic written report, including recommendations and any considerations for exam access arrangements.
  • This report will travel with the individual for life as I hold an APC.
Spotlight Assessment
  • Approximately 1.5 hours, this can be completed on children from age 6+
  • Assessment will cover standardised tests of underlying ability and one further area of cognitive processing, such as, phonological processing or working memory. A word reading and word spelling test will also be administered.
  • Detailed short written report, including recommendations.
  • Can be accompanied by a pupil focussed observation in class, for an additional fee.
Pupil Observation
  • Focus to be decided prior to observation.
  • Up to one hour observation in class.
  • Includes verbal feedback.
  • Optionally, written feedback can be provided for an additional charge.
Family & School Support
  • Sometimes support can come in the form of guidance and recommendations to the family/carers and staff supporting the individual in their journey.
  • Some examples include study skills, home routines to help improve home-life, and training in specific areas of need.
Specialist Teaching
  • Some individuals need their confidence and wellbeing boosted in relation to their learning.
  • Areas can be targeted such as literacy skills, study skills, emotional and mental wellbeing (although certified in Level 2 Counselling skills, I am not a counsellor).
  • My approach is holistic and does not separate learning from emotional and mental wellbeing.
  • Areas of focus are agreed beforehand.
  • Six week blocks also available at a cheaper rate.
Adult Learning
  • Registered as a Non-Medical Helper with the DSA.
  • Registered support worker with Access To Work.
  • Areas of targeted support agreed beforehand such as, study skills, organisation and planning time.
  • Emotional and mental wellbeing support available through the sessions and where it arises. Although I am certified in Level 2 Counselling Skills, I am not a counsellor. If it is apparent this would be advised, you will be signposted to counselling support.
